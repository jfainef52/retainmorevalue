The Core Shift

A retirement plan and a retirement income plan are two entirely different objectives.

⬆ Accumulation Phase — Going Up the Mountain
Volatility is manageable.
You are contributing regularly — dollar cost averaging works in your favor. A down market means more units purchased at lower prices. Time is your ally. The qualified plan excels here: tax deferral compounds every dollar.
Primary objectiveMaximize account balance
Volatility impactBuffered by contributions
Right toolQualified plan — maximize deferral
⬇ Distribution Phase — Coming Down the Mountain
Volatility is now the enemy.
Every distribution forces a sale. A bad year at the wrong time locks in permanent losses while income demands continue. The qualified plan becomes a liability: every dollar out is taxed as ordinary income, at the highest rate of your life.
Primary objectiveSustainable tax-efficient income
Volatility impactAmplified by forced distributions
Right toolNon-correlated tax-free asset
The Qualified Plan Problem
The same vehicle that deferred tax during accumulation now forces full ordinary income tax at distribution — exactly when sequence risk is at its peak.
The ETA Solution
Finance the tax obligation. Deploy the full distribution into a tax-free asset. Take income as asset loans — uncorrelated to the market, protected by a 0% floor, with no RMDs.

The Core Insight
The tax obligation exists regardless of what you do. The question is only whether you finance it at a fixed commercial rate — or pay it immediately at your full income tax rate. One costs 5–8%. The other costs 32–47%.
Why It Works
The full distribution — including the amount that would have gone to taxes — compounds inside a tax-free asset. The difference between what the asset earns and what the loan costs is the structural advantage.

Step by Step

The Enhanced Tax Advantage finances your tax obligation and deploys your full qualified plan balance into a tax-free compounding asset.

The Four Phases
1
Distribution & Deposit
The qualified plan distributes. The full amount — before tax — is deposited into a Premium Deposit Account (PDA). A bank simultaneously provides a loan to cover the tax obligation. Nothing is paid out of pocket.
2
Funding Phase (7 Annual Contributions)
The PDA funds annual contributions into a tax-free index life insurance asset over the funding period. The tax-free asset begins accumulating from day one.
3
Accumulation & Refinance
The tax-free asset accumulates — protected by a 0% floor and credited on a capped index. At the refinance year, the bank loan is retired via a participating asset loan at the carrier rate. The asset continues compounding on its full value.
4
Tax-Free Income Phase
Income is taken as participating policy loans — tax-free. The loan balance accrues at the carrier rate and retires from the death benefit. Income is optional and taken at your discretion. The IRS never sees it.
Five Structural Advantages
💰
Full Balance Goes to Work
The entire qualified plan balance — not the after-tax portion — compounds inside the tax-free asset. The amount that would have gone to the IRS now earns for you instead.
🛡️
0% Floor — No Down Years
When the index has a negative year, the asset credits 0% — not a negative return. The compounding engine never goes backward. Kai's problem is eliminated structurally.
📋
No RMDs — Ever
Life insurance is not subject to Required Minimum Distributions. Income is taken on your schedule — not the IRS's. You control the timing. You control the amount.
⚙️
Asset Value Unaffected by Income Loans
Policy loans do not reduce the policy value. The full value continues to compound — including on the loaned amount. Income does not impair the growth engine.

How We Model the Future

Not a projection. A probability distribution built from 50 years of real market history — applied to a 60/40 qualified plan and the ETA side by side.

How It Works
1
50 Years of Real S&P 500 Returns
The model draws from actual annual S&P 500 price returns spanning 1975–2024 — a pool that includes bull markets, bear markets, crashes, and recoveries. No theoretical distributions. Real history with real fat tails.
2
10,000 Bootstrapped Sequences
Each simulation draws a unique sequence of annual returns. Both the ETA and the qualified plan face the exact same sequence in each simulation — ensuring a fair, paired comparison. The same year that hurts the QP hurts the ETA, and vice versa.
3
ETA: 0% Guaranteed Floor / Index Cap Applied
ETA credits based on S&P 500 index performance each year, subject to a 0% guaranteed floor and full participation up to the cap. The 0% floor means the policy value never goes backward in a down year. All carrier charges from the actual illustration are applied year by year.
4
QP: 60/40 Blended Portfolio
The qualified plan uses a blended 60% equity / 40% bond portfolio return paired to the same historical year as the ETA. Full downside exposure — no floor.
Reading the Results
50th Percentile — Median
Half of all 10,000 simulated market sequences produced more income than this; half produced less. This is the base case — what a client in the middle of all possible market outcomes would experience. Both ETA and QP are shown at their respective 50th percentiles.
75th Percentile — Favorable
In 25% of simulated sequences — those with above-average market performance during the funding and income years — the ETA asset produced income and values above this level. This is not an optimistic assumption; it is a real probability.
25th Percentile — Conservative
In 75% of sequences the outcome exceeded this level. This is the stress-test floor — appropriate for conservative planning. Even at the 25th percentile, the ETA structure's 0% floor and tax-free income provide meaningful advantages over a taxable qualified plan distribution.
What This Is Not
This is not a guaranteed illustration. It is a probability-weighted model built from real market history. Results will differ from these projections. All values shown require a complete carrier illustration to be valid. This model is for discussion purposes only.
